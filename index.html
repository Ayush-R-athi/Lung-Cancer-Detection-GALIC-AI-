Common Symptoms of Lung Cancer

Persistent Cough

A chronic cough that does not go away is a common early sign of lung cancer.

Chest Pain

Unexplained pain or discomfort in the chest, often worsening with deep breaths or coughing.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing or a feeling of not getting enough air, even during normal activities.

Hoarseness

Changes in the voice, including hoarseness or a weakened voice, can be a warning sign.

Coughing Up Blood

Hemoptysis, or coughing up even a small amount of blood, should be evaluated immediately.

Fatigue & Weakness

Persistent tiredness and a general feeling of weakness that does not improve with rest.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Significant, unintended weight loss is a red flag and common in lung cancer cases.

Frequent Infections

Repeated episodes of bronchitis or pneumonia may indicate an underlying lung issue.

Bone Pain

Pain in the back or other bones may be a sign that lung cancer has spread.

Loss of Appetite

A sudden decrease in appetite and a general feeling of being unwell can occur in lung cancer.

Important: This AI system offers preliminary assessments. Always consult a medical professional for a confirmed diagnosis and treatment.
